Pizza

Pizza is an Italian dish typically consisting of a flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomato, cheese, and other ingredients, baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Ingredients:

    Dough:

  • Flour, water, yeast, salt, olive oil.

    • Sauce:

  • Tomato sauce with oregano, salt, and a pinch of sugar.

    • Toppings:

  • Mozzarella cheese (essential).

    • Options:

  • Ham, mushrooms, bell peppers, olives, pepperoni, basil, or arugula.
  1. Make the dough:

    2. Combine 3 cups of flour, 1 cup of warm water, 1 tsp salt, 1 tbsp olive oil, and 1 tsp yeast. Knead the dough for 10 minutes and let it rise for 1–2 hours.

  2. Prepare the sauce:

    3. Mix tomato sauce with oregano, salt, and a pinch of sugar.

  3. Assemble:

    4. Roll out the dough, place it on a baking sheet or pizza stone. Spread the sauce, add mozzarella cheese, and your chosen toppings.

  4. Cook:

    5. Bake at 220°C (425°F) for 10–15 minutes until the crust is crispy and cheese melts.
