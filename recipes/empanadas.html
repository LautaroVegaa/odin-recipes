Empanadas

An empanada is a type of baked or fried turnover consisting of pastry and filling, common in Spain, other Southern European countries, North African countries, Latin American countries, and the Philippines. The name comes from the Spanish empanar, and translates as 'breaded', that is, wrapped or coated in bread.

Ingredients:

    Dough:

  • Empanada pastry discs (or homemade dough with flour, water, salt, and butter/lard).

    • Filling (common options):

  • Ground beef or shredded chicken.
  • Onion, red bell pepper.
  • Hard-boiled egg.
  • Olives or raisins (optional).

    • Spices:

  • Paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.

    How to do it:

  1. Prepare the filling:

    2. Sauté chopped onion and bell pepper in a pan with a bit of oil. Add ground beef or shredded chicken and cook until browned. Season with paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix in chopped hard-boiled egg and olives or raisins if desired. Let the filling cool.

  2. Prepare the dough:

    3. Use store-bought discs or make your own by mixing flour, water, butter (or lard), and salt until you form a smooth dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes, then roll and cut into circles. Assemble: Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each disc. Fold the dough over and seal the edges by pinching or pressing with a fork.

  3. Cook:

    4. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 20–25 minutes, or fry until golden brown.
