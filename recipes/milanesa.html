Milanesas

The milanesa is a variation of the Lombard veal Milanese, or the Austrian Wiener schnitzel, where generic types of breaded cutlet preparations are known as a milanesa. The milanesa was brought to the Southern Cone by Italian immigrants between 1860 and the 1920s.

Ingredients:

  • Main Ingredient: Beef, chicken, or eggplant slices.

    • Breading:

  • Flour.
  • Eggs (beaten, seasoned with salt and pepper).
  • Breadcrumbs (seasoned with garlic powder, parsley, or parmesan cheese).
  • For Frying: Vegetable oil.
  • Optional Sides: Mashed potatoes, salad, or rice.
  1. Prepare the meat:

    2. Tenderize thin slices of beef or chicken. Season with salt and pepper.

  2. Bread the slices:

    3. Dredge in flour, dip in beaten eggs, and coat with breadcrumbs. Press firmly so breadcrumbs stick well.

  3. Cook:

    4. Fry in hot oil until golden brown on both sides, then drain on paper towels. Alternatively, bake at 200°C (400°F) for 15–20 minutes, flipping halfway for a healthier option.
